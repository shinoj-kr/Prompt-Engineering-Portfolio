### 1.1 Scenario A: Technical Explanation
#### Technical Concept : Blockchain

#### Initial Prompt: V1
Role Assignment : You are Andreas Antonopoulos, a world-renowned blockchain educator

Context Setting : Explain all important blockchain concepts starting from its origin.

Target Audience : 10 year old child.

Clear Goal : Target audience should be able to easily understand all the basic concepts of Blockchain.

Output Format : Narrative with examples.

Tone : Soft

#### Refined Prompt: V2
Role Assignment : You are Andreas Antonopoulos, a world-renowned blockchain educator

Context Setting : Explain all important blockchain concepts starting from its origin.

Target Audience : 10 year old child.

Clear Goal : Target audience should be able to easily understand all the basic concepts of Blockchain.

Output Format : Story and with a Copy button to copy the story told.

Tone : Soft like a father to his child

Length Constraints : Short & Sweet 

Positive Instructions : All the concepts should be arranged in such a way that it should be part of the story so that child won't feel bored and at the same time understand easily.

Negative Instructions : Concepts should not be arranged chapter wise like in school.

#### Refined Prompt: V3
Role Assignment:
You are Andreas Antonopoulos, a world-renowned blockchain educator and storyteller.

Context Setting:
Explain all important blockchain concepts starting from their origin and naturally progress toward modern blockchain applications.

Target Audience:
A curious 10-year-old child with no prior knowledge of blockchain or technology.

Clear Goal:
The child should easily understand the fundamental concepts of blockchain, remember them after reading the story, and be able to explain the basic idea of blockchain in their own words.

Output Format:

* Write as a short magical adventure story.
* Present the entire story inside a single copyable text block.
* Do not use headings, chapters, lessons, bullet points, tables, or school-style sections.
* Keep the story flowing naturally from beginning to end.

Creativity Level:
High. Use magical kingdoms, memorable characters, talking animals, enchanted books, treasure maps, magical crystals, or other imaginative elements while keeping the blockchain concepts accurate and easy to understand.

Tone:
Warm, gentle, encouraging, and caring—like a father telling a bedtime story to his child.

Length Constraint:
600–900 words.

Positive Instructions:

Naturally and seamlessly introduce the following concepts as part of the adventure:

* The problem of trust before blockchain existed
* Digital transactions
* Blocks
* Chain of blocks
* Distributed ledger
* Network of computers (nodes)
* Validation and consensus
* Cryptography (secret locks and keys)
* Immutability (records cannot be secretly changed)
* Transparency
* Security
* Mining or validation process
* Rewards
* Smart contracts
* Tokens and cryptocurrencies
* Real-world uses of blockchain

Ensure every concept becomes part of the story itself rather than being separately explained.

Story Flow Guidelines:

* Begin with a kingdom that struggles because nobody can fully trust transaction records.
* Introduce a magical solution that gradually becomes the blockchain.
* Show how magical records are stored in blocks and linked together into a chain.
* Let many magical guardians (nodes) work together to verify records.
* Include secret magical locks and keys to represent cryptography.
* Show why nobody can secretly alter old records.
* Introduce rewards for helping validate records.
* Include magical agreements that automatically execute when conditions are met (smart contracts).
* End with examples showing how the magical system helps people exchange value, track treasures, protect important records, and solve real-world problems.

End With:

* A very short summary connecting the story elements to all the actual blockchain concepts explained in the story.(4–7 sentences).
* A simple moral of the story.

Negative Instructions:

* Do NOT arrange concepts chapter-wise.
* Do NOT create school-like lessons.
* Do NOT use technical jargon without a child-friendly analogy.
* Do NOT break the story flow to explain definitions.
* Do NOT make the story feel like a textbook.
* Do NOT skip any of the listed blockchain concepts.
* Do NOT mention that you are teaching or explaining blockchain.

#### LLM Output to the Refined Prompt V3
Once upon a time, in the sparkling Kingdom of Lumaria, people loved trading wonderful things. Bakers traded sweet moon-cakes, farmers traded golden apples, and toy makers traded magical spinning tops. Every trade was written down in the Grand Record Book kept inside the castle.

At first, everyone trusted the book. But as the kingdom grew larger, problems began. One day a baker said, “I gave ten moon-cakes to the farmer!” The farmer replied, “No, it was only five!” Sometimes pages disappeared. Sometimes sneaky people secretly changed numbers at night. Soon, nobody was completely sure which records were true.

Far away in the Enchanted Crystal Forest lived a wise owl named Orion. He listened to the kingdom’s troubles and said, “What if no single person controlled the records? What if everyone helped protect them together?”

The king, queen, and villagers gathered to hear Orion’s idea.

In the center of the kingdom, Orion placed a glowing crystal. Whenever two people traded something, the crystal created a magical page describing the exchange. Instead of putting the page into one giant book, the crystal grouped many pages together into magical treasure boxes called blocks.

Each block held many trade stories. When one block became full, the crystal sealed it with a special magical mark and connected it to the previous block. Then a new block began. Soon there was a long line of connected blocks stretching across the kingdom like a shining chain.

The villagers called it the Chain of Blocks.

But who would keep track of all these blocks?

Orion had a clever answer.

Thousands of magical guardians appeared across the kingdom. Some were owls, some were foxes, some were rabbits, and some were even friendly dragons. Each guardian kept an identical copy of the entire chain. If someone wanted to see a record, they could ask any guardian.

Because every guardian held the same history, nobody needed to trust just one person anymore. The kingdom now shared a giant magical record book spread across many places at once.

Whenever a new trade happened, the guardians checked it carefully.

“Did Mia really own those golden apples?” asked one rabbit guardian.

“Did Leo actually send those silver stars?” asked a dragon guardian.

The guardians talked with one another until most of them agreed that the trade was real. Only then could the trade enter the next block.

This agreement among many guardians became known as the Great Harmony. No single guardian could decide alone. The network worked together.

To keep trades safe, every villager received a magical key and a magical lock.

The lock was public, so anyone could send treasures to it. But only the owner’s secret key could open it.

If Emma wanted to send five silver stars to Noah, she used her secret key to create a magical signature. The guardians could see that the signature matched Emma’s lock without ever learning her secret key. This clever magic protected everyone from thieves.

One evening, a sneaky goblin tried to change an old trade record.

He opened a block from many months ago and changed “5 silver stars” into “500 silver stars.”

But the moment he did, the magical mark sealing that block changed. Then the next block no longer matched. Then the next one. And the next one.

Soon every guardian noticed.

“That record has been tampered with!” shouted the foxes.

Because every block was connected to the ones before and after it, secretly changing old records became nearly impossible. The chain protected its own history.

As years passed, the guardians continued checking new blocks. The work required effort, patience, and magical energy. To thank them, the crystal rewarded honest guardians with shining Star Tokens.

These tokens became valuable across Lumaria. People traded them for goods and services. Some villagers saved them. Others spent them. Before long, Star Tokens became a trusted magical currency.

One day, Orion introduced an even more amazing invention.

He created enchanted scrolls that could make promises automatically.

A scroll might say, “If the treasure chest arrives at the harbor, pay the sailor ten Star Tokens.”

Nobody needed to remind the scroll. Nobody could secretly stop it. The moment the condition became true, the scroll carried out the agreement exactly as written.

Merchants loved these magical agreements.

Farmers used them. Sailors used them. Builders used them. Everyone trusted them because they followed their instructions perfectly.

As Lumaria prospered, the magical chain found many uses beyond money.

Treasure hunters tracked ancient treasures so nobody could claim a treasure they never found.

Doctors stored important healing records safely.

Artists proved ownership of their beautiful paintings.

Schools protected important certificates.

Charities showed exactly where donated coins traveled.

Because everyone could see the records, the kingdom became more transparent. Because thousands of guardians protected the chain, it became secure. Because old records could not be secretly rewritten, people trusted the history.

Many years later, children would gather around Orion and ask how the kingdom became so peaceful.

The wise owl would smile and point toward the shining chain stretching across the horizon.

“It works,” he would say, “because nobody owns the truth alone. Everyone protects it together.”

And beneath the moonlit sky, the chain continued growing, one block at a time, carrying the story of the kingdom safely into the future.

In the story, the kingdom’s trust problem represents life before blockchain. The magical blocks connected together form the blockchain. The guardians are the network of computers, called nodes, that validate records through consensus. The magical locks and secret keys represent cryptography that keeps transactions secure. The connected blocks make records difficult to change, creating immutability and transparency. Star Tokens represent cryptocurrencies, and the enchanted scrolls represent smart contracts. Together, they show how blockchain can help exchange value, track assets, protect records, and solve real-world problems.

Moral: When many honest helpers work together and protect the truth, trust becomes stronger than any single ruler or record book.

#### Refined Prompt: V3
Role Assignment:
You are a Professional Business Communication Expert specializing in corporate email writing and workplace etiquette.

Context Setting:
The candidate has received a job offer but has decided not to accept it due to personal career considerations. The candidate wishes to decline the offer respectfully while maintaining a positive relationship with the company.

Target Audience:
Human Resources Manager

Clear Goal:
Draft a professional email that politely declines the job offer, expresses gratitude for the opportunity, and conveys continued interest in future opportunities with the company.

Output Format:
Email

Tone:
Professional, Courteous, and Positive

Length Constraint:
150-200 words

Positive Instructions: 
Mention a positive and professional reason for declining the offer without criticizing the company.

Constraints and Rules
Include a professional closing statement.

Negative Instructions:

* Do not use rude, negative, or emotional language.
* Do not disclose personal or confidential details.
* Do not compare the company with other employers.
* Do not burn bridges or imply dissatisfaction with the company.
* Do not use informal language, slang, or emojis.

#### LLM Output to the Refined Prompt: V3
Dear Hiring Manager,

I hope you are doing well.

Thank you very much for offering me the opportunity to join your organization. I sincerely appreciate the time and effort invested by you and the interview panel throughout the recruitment process. It was a pleasure learning more about the company, its culture, and the exciting work being undertaken by the team.
After careful consideration of my current career goals and long-term professional plans, I have decided not to proceed with the offer at this time. This decision was not easy, as I hold your organization in high regard and am grateful for the confidence you demonstrated in my abilities.
I would like to express my sincere appreciation for your consideration and professionalism throughout the process. I remain impressed by the organization and would welcome the opportunity to be considered for suitable roles in the future should our paths align again.
Thank you once again for your understanding. I wish you and the team continued success.

Kind regards,

[Your Name]

## Part 3: Strategic Prompting Techniques
### 3.1 Chain-of-Thought Prompting
### Option B: Multi-Step Reasoning
### 1. Without Chain-of-Thought: Write a prompt that asks for just the answer
#### Prompt
Problem: A company has 150 employees. 60% work in engineering, 25% in sales,
and the rest in operations. If the company plans to hire 20% more engineers
and 10% more sales people, how many total employees will there be after hiring?

Give me only the final answer

#### LLM Output Without Chain-of-Thought
171.75 employees

### 2. With Chain-of-Thought: Write a prompt that instructs the LLM to think step-by-step
#### Prompt
Problem: A company has 150 employees. 60% work in engineering, 25% in sales,
and the rest in operations. If the company plans to hire 20% more engineers
and 10% more sales people, how many total employees will there be after hiring?

Solve the above problem step by step

#### LLM Output With Chain-of-Thought
#### Step 1: Find the number of employees in each department

* Engineering = 60% of 150 = **0.60 × 150 = 90**
* Sales = 25% of 150 = **0.25 × 150 = 37.5**
* Operations = 150 − 90 − 37.5 = **22.5**

#### Step 2: Calculate new hires

* Engineering hires = 20% of 90 = **0.20 × 90 = 18**
* Sales hires = 10% of 37.5 = **0.10 × 37.5 = 3.75**

#### Step 3: Find the new department totals

* Engineering after hiring = 90 + 18 = **108**
* Sales after hiring = 37.5 + 3.75 = **41.25**
* Operations remains = **22.5**

#### Step 4: Calculate the total employees after hiring

108 + 41.25 + 22.5 = **171.75**

#### Final Answer

**171.75 employees** (approximately **172 employees** if rounded to a whole number).

### 3. Comparison 

### How the outputs differed

The output without Chain-of-Thought provided only the final answer, which was 171.75 employees. The output with Chain-of-Thought showed each calculation step, including the number of employees in each department, the additional hires, and the final total. This made the reasoning process transparent and easier to follow. The final answer remained the same in both outputs. 

### Why chain-of-thought helps with complex reasoning

Chain-of-Thought helps by breaking a complex problem into smaller and more manageable steps. It allows the model to perform calculations systematically instead of jumping directly to the final answer. This reduces the chance of errors in multi-step problems involving percentages and arithmetic operations. It also helps users understand how the final answer was derived. 

### One limitation you observed

A limitation of Chain-of-Thought prompting is that it produces longer responses compared to direct answers. Even though the reasoning is shown step by step, the model can still make mistakes in its calculations or assumptions. The additional explanation may also be unnecessary when only the final answer is required. Therefore, Chain-of-Thought improves transparency but does not guarantee correctness.

### 4.3 Limitations & Responsible Use

## Three Limitations Encountered While Working with LLMs

1. LLMs can sometimes provide incorrect or misleading information even when the response appears confident.
2. The quality of the output depends heavily on the quality and clarity of the prompt provided.
3. LLMs may not always fully understand context and can occasionally misinterpret ambiguous instructions.

## Three Recommendations for Responsible LLM Use

1. Important information generated by an LLM should always be verified using reliable sources before use.
2. LLMs should not be relied upon as the sole source for critical decisions involving medical, legal, financial, or safety-related matters.
3. LLMs should be used ethically by respecting privacy, avoiding plagiarism, and reviewing outputs for fairness, accuracy, and potential bias.
